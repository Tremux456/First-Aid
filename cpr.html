First Aid for CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

CPR is a life-saving technique used in emergencies when someone's heart has stopped beating or they're not breathing. It involves chest compressions and rescue breaths to help keep blood and oxygen flowing to vital organs.:

    When to Use CPR:
  1. Cardiac Arrest: When the heart stops beating.
  2. Drowning.
  3. Severe, life-threatening injuries.
  4. Electric shock.
    Steps for CPR (for adults and children over 8 years old):
  1. Check Responsiveness:

    2. Tap the person and shout, "Are you okay?" If there's no response, proceed.

  2. Call for Help:

    3. Shout for someone to call emergency services or do it yourself if you're alone.

  3. Position Hands: Place the heel of one hand on the center of the chest

    4. Place the heel of one hand on the center of the chest (between the nipples) and place your other hand on top, interlocking your fingers.

  4. Chest Compressions:

    5. *Push down hard and fast, at least 2 inches deep, and at a rate of 100-120 compressions per minute.
    * Allow the chest to rise completely between compressions.

  5. Give Rescue Breaths:

    6. * Tilt the person's head back and lift the chin.
    * Pinch the nose shut and make a complete seal over their mouth with yours.
    * Give 2 breaths, each lasting about 1 second, watching for the chest to rise.
    * Continue with 30 compressions followed by 2 breaths (30:2 ratio) until help arrives or the person starts to breathe on their own.
