Introduction to First Aid for Electrical Burns

Electrical burns occur when an electrical current passes through the body, causing tissue damage both externally and internally. These burns can be deceptive, as the external injury might not reflect the internal damage.

Common Causes:

  1. Contact with exposed electrical wires
  2. Electrical outlets
  3. Lightning strikesPesticides and fertilizers

First Aid Steps:

  1. Call emergency services (112).

    2. Electrical burns can cause severe internal injuries, so it's essential to get immediate medical help.

  2. Ensure Safety:

    3. Do not touch the person if they are still in contact with the electrical source. Turn off the power source or use a non-conductive object (like a wooden stick) to move them away from the source.

  3. Check for Breathing and Pulse:

    4. If the person is not breathing or has no pulse, begin CPR if you are trained to do so.

  4. Cover the Burn:

    5. Cover any visible burns with a clean, sterile bandage or cloth. Do not apply ointments or creams

  5. Monitor for Shock:

    6. Keep the person lying down and elevate their legs slightly. Cover them with a blanket to keep them warm until medical help arrives.

OTHER LEVELS
* Chemical Burns :
can happen when the skin comes into contact with hazardous substances like cleaning agents, bleach, or acid.

* Electrical Burns:
can be caused by contact with an electrical current and may not always be visible on the skin.
