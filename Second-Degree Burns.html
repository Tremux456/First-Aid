Introduction to First Aid for Second-Degree Burns

Second-degree burns, also known as partial-thickness burns, affect both the outer layer of the skin (epidermis) and the underlying layer (dermis). These burns can cause blistering, severe pain, redness, and swelling. Here's how to provide first aid for a second-degree burn:

Click for Translation

Michomo ya digrii ya pili, pia inajulikana kama kuungua kwa unene wa sehemu, kuathiri safu ya nje ya ngozi (epidermis) na safu ya msingi (dermis). Kuungua huku kunaweza kusababisha malengelenge, maumivu makali, uwekundu, na uvimbe. Hivi ndivyo jinsi ya kutoa huduma ya kwanza kwa kuchoma kwa kiwango cha pili:

  • Cool the Burn:

    • Similar to first-degree burns, run cool (not cold) water over the burn for 15-20 minutes. Do not use ice as it can cause further damage to the tissue.

    Click for Translation

    Sawa na kuchomwa kwa kiwango cha kwanza, endesha maji baridi (sio baridi) juu ya kuchoma kwa dakika 15-20. Usitumie barafu kwani inaweza kusababisha uharibifu zaidi kwa tishu.

  • Avoid Breaking Blisters:

    • If blisters form, do not break them. They help protect the underlying skin from infection.

    Click for Translation

    Ikiwa malengelenge yanaunda, usiyavunje. Wanasaidia kulinda ngozi ya msingi kutokana na maambukizi.

  • Clean the Area:

    • Gently clean the burn with mild soap and water. Pat the area dry with a clean cloth.

    Click for Translation

    Safisha kwa upole kidonda kwa sabuni na maji kidogo. Pat eneo kavu na kitambaa safi.

  • Apply a Clean Bandage:

    • Use a sterile, non-stick bandage to cover the burn. This helps prevent infection and protects the area.

    Click for Translation

    Tumia bandeji tasa, isiyo na fimbo ili kufunika kidonda. Hii husaidia kuzuia maambukizi na kulinda eneo.

  • Apply an Antibiotic Ointment:

    • Applying a thin layer of antibiotic ointment can help prevent infection and promote healing.

    Click for Translation

    Kutumia safu nyembamba ya marashi ya viuavijasumu kunaweza kusaidia kuzuia maambukizi na kukuza uponyaji.

  • Pain Relief:

    • Over-the-counter pain relievers like ibuprofen or acetaminophen can help manage pain.

    Click for Translation

    Vipunguza maumivu vya dukani kama vile ibuprofen au acetaminophen vinaweza kusaidia kudhibiti maumivu.

  • Monitor for Infection:

    • Similar to first-degree burns, run cool (not cold) water over the burn for 15-20 minutes. Do not use ice as it can cause further damage to the tissue.

    Click for Translation

    Sawa na kuchomwa kwa kiwango cha kwanza, endesha maji baridi (sio baridi) juu ya kuchoma kwa dakika 15-20. Usitumie barafu kwani inaweza kusababisha uharibifu zaidi kwa tishu.

  • Seek Medical Attention if Necessary:

    • If the burn covers a large area, is on the face, hands, feet, or groin, or if there are signs of infection, seek medical attention promptly.

    Click for Translation

    Ikiwa kuungua kunafunika eneo kubwa, ni juu ya uso, mikono, miguu, au kinena, au ikiwa kuna dalili za kuambukizwa, tafuta matibabu mara moja.

    REMEMBER
    • Remember, it is crucial to monitor the healing process and consult with a healthcare professional if you have any concerns.
