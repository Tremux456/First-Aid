First Aid for Choking

Choking happens when an object gets stuck in the throat or windpipe, blocking the flow of air. This can be really dangerous because it cuts off oxygen to the brain.

Click for Translation

# Choking hufanyika wakati kitu kinakwama kwenye koo au bomba la upepo, kuzuia mtiririko wa hewa. Hii inaweza kuwa hatari kwa sababu hupunguza oksijeni kwa ubongo.

    Signs of choking
  1. Difficulty breathing or noisy breathing.
  2. Coughing (may be weak or forceful).
  3. Skin, lips, and nails turning blue or gray.
  4. Loss of consciousness.
    First aid steps for adults and children over 1 year old
  1. Encourage to Cough:
    2.

    If the person is coughing, encourage them to keep coughing to try to clear the blockage

    Click for Translation

    # Ikiwa mtu anakohoa, muhimize kuendelee kukohoa ili kujaribu kusafisha kilichobana

  2. Back Blows:
    3.

    If coughing doesn't work, perform 5 strong back blows. Stand to the side and slightly behind the person, lean them forward, and use the heel of your hand to give firm blows between their shoulder blades.

    Click for Translation

    # Ikiwa kukohoa haifanyi kazi, fanya makofi matano katika mgongo kwa nguvu. Simama upande na nyuma kidogo ya mtu, na utumie kisigino cha mkono wako kutoa makofi thabiti kati ya blade zao za bega.

  3. Abdominal Thrusts (Heimlich Maneuver):
    4.

    If back blows fail, perform 5 abdominal thrusts. Stand behind the person, place your arms around their waist, make a fist with one hand, place it just above their navel, grab your fist with your other hand, and thrust sharply inwards and upwards3.

    Click for Translation

    # Ikiwa milipuko ya nyuma inashindwa, fanya vifurushi 5 vya tumbo. Simama nyuma ya mtu, weka mikono yako kiunoni, Tengeneza ngumi kwa mkono mmoja, weka juu ya kitovu chao, Kunyakua ngumi yako kwa mkono wako mwingine, na kusukuma kwa kasi ndani na zaidi 3

    For infants under 1 year old
  1. Back Blows:

    2. Lay the baby face-down along your forearm, holding their head, and deliver 5 gentle but firm back thumps, using the heel of your hand

    Click for Translation

    # Weka uso wa mtoto chini ya mkono wako, Kwa kushikilia kichwa chake, na kutoa mapigo matano ya upole lakini thabiti, Kutumia mkono wako

  2. Chest Thrusts:

    3. Lay the baby face-down along your forearm, holding their head, and deliver 5 gentle but firm back thumps, using the heel of your hand

    NOTEL: When to call for emergency help!
  1. If the person is still choking after trying back blows and abdominal thrusts, call emergency services immediately
  2. If the person becomes unconscious, call for emergency help and start CPR
