First Aid for Small Cuts

Introduction

Small cuts are the minor injuries where the skin is broken but the wound is shallow, not bleeding excessively, and do not expose the deeper tissues.

When performing first aid the following sets you should follow them.

  • Wash your Hands

    • * Clean your hands with soap and water or use hand sanitizer to avoid infection between you and the victim

  • Stop the Bleeding.

    • * Appy gentle pressure using a clean cloth or sterile gauze. Hold for few minutesnuntil the bleeding stops

  • Clean the Wound.

    • * Rise the cut under cool running water to remove dedris. Use mild soap around the area not directly inside the cut (IF NEEDED USE)

  • Apply an Antibiotic Ointiment.

    • * Gently apply a thin layer of antiseptic or Antibiotic Ointiment like Neosporin to prevent infection.

  • Cover the Wound.

    • * Use a clean adhesive bandage or sterile dressing.
    You should replace it daily or when it becomes wet/dirty.

  • Monitor for infection.

    • * Watch for redness, swelling orpus, which may indicate an infection

    NOTE
    • Avoid touching the wound directly to prevent infection
    • Do not use unlcean materials(e.g., tissues, unwashed cloth) on the wound.
    • Check tetanus vaccination status if the cut is caused by dirty or rust object
