Introduction to First Aid for Chemical Burns

Chemical burns occur when the skin or eyes come into contact with harmful chemicals, such as acids, alkalis, or corrosive substances. These burns can be serious and require immediate attention

Common Causes:

  1. Household cleaners (bleach, ammonia)
  2. Industrial chemicals (acids, alkalis)
  3. Pesticides and fertilizers

First Aid Steps:

  1. Call emergency services (112).
  2. Remove the Chemical Source:

    3. Carefully remove any contaminated clothing and rinse the affected area with cool running water for at least 20 minutes. Ensure to protect yourself from contamination.

  3. Flush the Area:

    4. Continue to flush the burned area with cool water to remove any remaining chemicals. Avoid using harsh or scented soaps, which can further irritate the skin.

  4. Cover the Burn:

    5. Use a clean, sterile bandage or cloth to cover the burn. Avoid applying ointments or creams as they can trap chemicals in the skin.

  5. Seek Medical Attention:

    6. It's crucial to get professional medical help to assess the severity of the burn and provide appropriate treatment.

OTHER LEVELS
* Chemical Burns :
can happen when the skin comes into contact with hazardous substances like cleaning agents, bleach, or acid.

* Electrical Burns:
can be caused by contact with an electrical current and may not always be visible on the skin.
