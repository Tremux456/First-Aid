Introduction to First Aid for First-Degree Burns

First-degree burns, also known as superficial burns, affect only the outer layer of the skin. They typically result in redness, pain, and minor swelling. Here are the steps for providing first aid for a first-degree burn:

Click for Translation

# Burns ya kiwango cha kwanza, pia inajulikana kama kuchoma juu, huathiri tu safu ya nje ya ngozi. Kwa kawaida husababisha uwekundu, maumivu, na uvimbe mdogo. Hapa kuna hatua za kutoa msaada wa kwanza kwa kuchoma kwa kiwango cha kwanza:

  • Cool the Burn:

    • Run cool (not very cold) water over the burn for about 10-15 minutes. Avoid using ice, as it can damage the skin further.

    Click for Translation

    # tumia maji baridi (sio baridi sana) maji juu ya kidonda kwa dakika 10-15. Epuka kutumia barafu, kwani inaweza kuharibu ngozi zaidi.

  • Clean the Area:

    • Gently clean the burn with mild soap and water. Be careful not to scrub the area.

    Click for Translation

    # Safisha kidonda kwa upole na sabuni kali na maji. Kuwa mwangalifu usichubue eneo hilo.

  • Apply a Clean Bandage:

    • Use a sterile, non-stick bandage to cover the burn. This helps prevent infection and keeps the area clean.

    Click for Translation

    # Tumia bandage isiyo shika, isiyo na fimbo kufunika kidonda. Hii husaidia kuzuia maambukizi na kuweka eneo safi.

  • Pain Relief:

    • You can take over-the-counter pain relievers like ibuprofen or acetaminophen to manage pain. Avoid breaking any blisters that may form.

    Click for Translation

    # Unaweza kuchukua dawa za maumivu kama ibuprofen au acetaminophen

  • Moisturize the Skin:

    • After cooling and covering the burn, you can apply a smoothing lotion or aloe vera gel to keep the skin moist and aid healing.

    Click for Translation

    # Baada ya kupoa na kufunika kuchoma, Unaweza kutumia lotion laini au Aloe vera gel kuweka ngozi unyevu na uponyaji wa msaada.
